Prepare To Be Mined

December 9th, 2017

It is most likely fitting that I listen to the foreboding sounds of Trent Reznor and Atticus Ross on their absolutely gorgeous yet haunting soundtrack they did for David Fincher's take on The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo. I only say that because it is Dark O'Clock on a Saturday evening and the view outside yields fresh snow. Somewhat fresh. I mean, it has been steadily coming down all day. While beautiful, I still can't help myself from going into that thought of "is this a harbringer of something bigger aroudn the corner?" I blame Trent, Atticus, and the gin.

So, Job Hunt 2017, soon to spill into 2018. What can I say? I'm trying to keep this chin up but I'm thinking I came to New York at the wrong time of the year for job searching. No, no I didn't. I came at precisely the right time. I'm deciding to put the positivity out there, not the doubt. So where do I stand? Well, Danielle had mentioned a friend of hers who just got hired with a custom/bespoke lighting company in Soho and suggested I apply for a position there. Which I did. Her friend responded right away to my application, led me to an online assesment test (to determine if I'm capable) and had me schedule a phone screen. I spoke with him Friday afternoon and felt it went well! Then again, the last three phone chats I had I've felt went well and they all turned up bumpkiss. As Andrea and Lauren both told me, it's all about managing expectations, or, learning to not be disappointed until you sign the damn papers. *sigh* I'm still holding out hope.

TL:DR

Let it snow.

“Into the arms of anyone that hovers in this general space, these are the sort of random things that make life interesting” — Rob Crow

Not much else really to talk about except a new series Danielle and I started on Netflix. It is titled Dark and I have to say, its a bit addictive. Think of a mix of Stranger Things and True Detective Season 1 (not that Season 2 mess). It is a German show so it is redubbed for English audiences but they should have let actually Americans read the dialogue. The translations are fine enough, they just sound like someone who doesn't speak American English. Conversationally it sounds a bit strange. But that's a nit-pick.

Friday night, Danielle and I made tacos and drank some wine. Not a typical comfort food but that meal did so much to quench some deep seeded memories that both of us hold onto. Something about that cheap taco seasoning mix that brough us both back to great moments of younger days gone by.

Shuffled around in the snow a bit this afternoon and I'm thiking I should have checked a larger suitcase. I'm maintianing right now but HOLY COW I NEED NEW OUTFITS! *sigh* First world problems, I know, and after this shitty week of our inept leadership and So Cal completely being decimated, I can't really complain about anything. Wish I had more for y'all...
