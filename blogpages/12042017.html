Supermoon

December 4th, 2017

SATURDAY: Rode the train out to Queens to meet up with Andrea since she had won some tickets through her work to see a show in Newark. The show? SAD! A Happy Evening with Stephen Colbert and Samantha Bee. Yeah, they were part of a fundraising event put on by Montclair Film and it was GLORIOUS! Basically, Stephen and Samantha sat on stage and had a conversation about her career, motherhood, politics, and the current state of disarray this country is in. A wonderfully candid and humorous event in which they chatted for about an hour and a half and then opened the floor for questions from the audience. Quite a journey to get to Newark and this past weekend, MTA did its best to make sure we wouldn't make it out there. Gotta love the transit here. Sometimes its the best invention since sliced bread and other times its the worst invention since slice bread. I think the locals call it "getting New Yorked".

SUNDAY: Sat around most of the day watching documentaries and making fried rice. Yeah. Fried Rice. I'm pretty effing good at it. My recipe is frying up some garlic and onions in canola/vegetable oil with a small amount of sesame oil. You can put sesame seeds in there but the oil gives it that little kick. Add some frozen peas and carrots, and I added some chicken and smoked almond pieces. Push it all to the side while you fry a couple of eggs in there. Put a fair amount of soy sauce (tamari for my GF friends) and toss all the mixture together to get it nice and coated. Then stir in some jasmine rice. NOM NOM. Sure, its not as glamourous or as tasty as a restaurant's but it suites my taste (literally) and is pretty cheap to make large amounts.

Totally forgot about the supermoon but happened to catch a bit of it above the line of houses across the street. Tried my best to capture it (*SPOILER ALERT* I didn't) and that's what this post's banner picture is. By the time I saw it (Approximately 8:30pm EST), it was more of a harvest moon, with a pumpkin-y orange and a size more akin to a "run-of-the-mill moon" (Confidential to the moon: You're always "super" in my eyes). With the slight bit of clouds and foggy air, I thought the moon had somehow transported me back to Halloween.
